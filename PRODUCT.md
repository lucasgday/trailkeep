# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Developers on macOS and Linux who use one or more AI coding tools and need a durable, private way to revisit decisions, implementation history, and unfinished context. They use trailkeep locally, often across long-running projects, and expect the archive to stay understandable even after the source tool changes or removes its own history.

## Product Purpose

trailkeep turns private, vendor-specific coding-agent sessions into a cumulative local Markdown archive and a browsable bilingual viewer. Success means users can trust that captured history remains on their machine, find the conversation or project context they need, understand how work unfolded, and resume it without vendor lock-in or silent data loss.

## Brand Personality

Private, trustworthy, and pragmatic. The voice is direct and honest about limitations, technically precise without becoming cryptic, calm under dense information, and equally considered in English and Spanish.

## Anti-references

- Cloud-first builder profiles that upload raw or derived activity.
- Telemetry-driven dashboards or interfaces that hide what data leaves the machine.
- Dense, generic admin dashboards that flatten conversations into disconnected metrics.
- Decorative AI-product chrome, novelty interactions, and visual effects that compete with reading.
- Browser-native product controls or inconsistent local lookalikes that make the standalone viewer feel unreliable.

## Design Principles

1. Make privacy observable: every feature must preserve the zero-network viewer and local-only data flow.
2. Preserve the narrative: show user intent, agent work, tools, and related sub-work in the context where they happened.
3. Prefer cumulative truth over cleanup: never trade away captured history for a tidier presentation.
4. Reveal density progressively: keep scanning calm, then expose detail inline when the user asks for it.
5. Earn trust through consistency: reuse the viewer's established controls, source language, metadata, and evidence patterns.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Keep all product controls keyboard-operable with visible focus, practical touch targets, readable contrast, and state cues that do not rely on color alone. Respect reduced-motion preferences, preserve useful behavior at narrow widths, and maintain complete English and Spanish UI parity.
